PCB Foundation

  2. PCB Foundation
The “Patrick and Carol Bitature Foundation”, is a non-profit organization, working towards providing adequate healthcare and opportunities to disadvantaged youth in communities all over Uganda for over 10 years.
Our flagship programme was Project 500k which is a community based educational initiative that is equipping 500,000 youth with knowledge and skills in financial literacy as well as the attitudes and behaviours associated with successful entrepreneurship.
Since then, we have grown to support numerous programmes and causes. Our full list of causes are:
Cure Hospital

I currently serve as the Co-Chair of the Board of Directors for Cure Hospital, an international organisation serving children with conditions like clubfoot, bowed legs, cleft lips, untreated burns, and hydrocephalus. Without treatment, they will have little hope for a future. Many will die from their condition.

Ibanda Community Organization Network(ICON)

This initiative aims at attaining a self-sustaining community through various Local, Economic and Development Interventions with a view to eradicate poverty and enhance Social-Economic transformation of the Communities.

Project 500k

Project 500k was born out of my passion to do something that outlives me. Having had many discussions with friends and family, we launched it intending to touch the lives of 500,000 Ugandan youth in a structured way that we can measure. We do this by either, helping them with their entrepreneurial journey, job search or giving them a mind shift.


In partnership with PSFU and Mastercard Foundation, Protea Marriott Hotel Kampala started an initiative to jumpstart the tourism industry which has been drastically affected by the Covid 19 pandemic. The initiative's focus is to boost those in the catering and local crafts industry, stimulate job creation, empower and support them with the necessary skills.
